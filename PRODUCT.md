# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

APAC B2B tech / SaaS founders and operators — Singapore, India, Australia / NZ — typically Series A–B, with proven product-market fit at home and an active (or imminent) push into the US and European markets. They are technical and systems-minded: they trust data, specificity, and working artifacts over agency adjectives. The job they're hiring GTMx for: stand up a repeatable outbound + RevOps engine that produces qualified Western pipeline **before** they burn runway on a premature US sales hire or a copied APAC playbook.

The site itself is read in evaluation mode — a founder deciding "do I build this engine, hire for it, or outsource it?" Shipped messaging has broadened slightly beyond the APAC-only framing (meta copy says "B2B tech companies"), but the core ICP and the unfair advantage — operators who've sold across APAC and run GTM for YC-backed US companies — remain the strategic anchor.

## Product Purpose

GTMx is a done-for-you GTM engineering agency. It designs, builds, and *runs* three connected systems that turn a working product into predictable pipeline:

- **Automated Outbound** — cold email + LinkedIn, infrastructure to copy to managed campaigns
- **RevOps** — Clay / HubSpot builds: sourcing, enrichment, scoring, routing, automated
- **Search** — the third pillar of the engine

Business success is booked, qualified meetings and pipeline. The **website's** job (per the owner) is *authority + inbound*: build credibility through case studies and content so the right founders arrive already convinced, with the Cal.com 30-min GTM Audit as the single capture action. Conversion is the floor; trust is the lever.

## Brand Personality

Engineered, candid, warm. Builder-to-builder voice — direct, no fluff, allergic to consulting-speak. It speaks in systems ("build, engineer, engine, infrastructure, pipeline, iterate") and refuses the filler ("strategy, leverage, synergy, holistic, end-to-end" as empty words). The visual personality adds the human counterweight: the playful "daydream" palette and hand-drawn Caveat scribbles signal that real people — not a faceless lead-gen vendor — build and run this. The emotional target: *confident relief* — "these people have actually done this, and they'll show me the work."

## Anti-references

- **Generic SaaS-cream agency template** — timid warm-white background, one accent, identical feature cards. The default this site must out-personality.
- **Corporate consultancy** — navy-and-gold, stock handshakes, "diverse team in a meeting," holistic-synergy copy.
- **Dark terminal / hacker cliché** — the obvious "engineering tool" reflex (the original brief's instinct; deliberately abandoned).
- **Cheap lead-gen vendor** — "blast 10k emails" energy. GTMx engineers systems; it is not a list-blasting service.
- **Hero-metric template** — big number / small label / gradient accent repeated as the whole personality.

## Design Principles

1. **Show the work, not the promise.** Favor real artifacts — mini-mockups, named methods, actual numbers — over adjectives. The proof IS the pitch.
2. **Builder-to-builder candor.** Specific, plain, confident. If a sentence could appear on any agency site, cut it.
3. **Engineered, but human.** Systems rigor (clean structure, real data) carried by hand-drawn warmth (scribbles, candy accents). Neither alone is the brand.
4. **Earned proof over borrowed authority.** Lead with what GTMx has actually built and shipped; let case studies and method do the persuading.
5. **One reader, spoken to directly.** Every section addresses the expanding APAC-tech founder, not a generic "you."

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No formal conformance bar is required. Apply good judgment: keep body and label text legible against tinted/peach surfaces (watch muted `--ink-soft` on light fills), keep tap targets generous (many founders read mobile-first), and don't gate content visibility on scroll-reveal JS. Don't over-engineer beyond this.
