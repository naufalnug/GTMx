# GTMx Website Redesign Brief
### Instruction document for Claude Code — APAC-to-West positioning overhaul

## Context & Business Background

**Company:** GTMx  
**Website:** www.gtmx.run  
**What we do:** We are a done-for-you GTM engineering agency that helps APAC B2B tech companies build the outbound revenue systems they need to win their first customers in the US and Europe.  
**Who we serve:** Series A–B B2B tech/SaaS companies based in Singapore, India, and Australia/NZ that are actively expanding (or preparing to expand) into the US and European markets.  
**Our unfair advantage:** Our founders have 8+ years of B2B sales experience across APAC and Europe, plus hands-on GTM execution experience with YC-backed companies in the US. We understand both sides of the expansion equation — the APAC founder's world and the Western buyer's expectations.  
**Core belief:** APAC companies don't fail in the US because of their product. They fail because they copy APAC playbooks into Western markets, or they hire a US VP of Sales before they have a repeatable process. GTMx builds the engine first.

---

## The Redesign Goal

The current website needs to be rewritten and restructured to speak **exclusively to APAC B2B tech founders** who are thinking about or actively pursuing US/EU expansion. Every section, every headline, every CTA must speak to this one person.

The tone is: **direct, engineering-minded, no fluff.** This audience are technical founders and operators. They respond to systems, data, and specificity — not vague consulting language.

**Words to use:** build, engineer, system, engine, infrastructure, pipeline, workflow, execute, deploy, iterate  
**Words to avoid:** strategy, advise, consult, solution, leverage, synergy, holistic, end-to-end

Use this as the north star for all copy decisions:

## Design & Technical Directives

### Colour & Visual Tone
- Lean into a **dark, technical aesthetic** — think dashboard, terminal, engineering tool rather than a marketing agency
- Accent colour should feel precise and modern: electric blue (`#3B82F6`), green (`#10B981`), or sharp orange (`#F97316`) — pick one and be consistent
- Avoid warm, friendly pastels or anything that reads "creative agency"
- Typography should be clean and modern — Inter, Geist, or similar sans-serif

### Imagery & Icons
- No generic stock photography (no handshakes, no "diverse team in a meeting")
- Use abstract technical visuals: pipeline flow diagrams, outbound sequence visualisations, world maps with APAC→US/EU connection lines
- Icons should be minimal and line-based, not colourful or illustrative

### Mobile
- Fully responsive — many APAC founders will view on mobile first
- CTAs must be large and tappable on mobile
- Pricing cards should stack cleanly on small screens

### Performance
- Keep third-party scripts minimal — page speed matters for SEO in APAC markets
- All CTA buttons should link to a calendar booking tool (Calendly or equivalent)

## Summary of Priority Order for Claude Code

If implementing in stages, prioritise in this order:

1. **Hero section** — this is the highest-impact change. Get the headline and CTA right first.
2. **Problem section** — this is your hook. Founders need to see themselves in this immediately.
3. **Who it's for** — the ICP checklist qualifies visitors and filters out the wrong leads.
4. **How it works** — reduces friction for interested founders.
5. **Packages** — makes the commercial offer clear.
6. **Social proof / objections** — builds trust and closes hesitation.
7. **SEO meta tags** — implement last but don't skip.
