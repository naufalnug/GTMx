Prepared for Latitude

Latitude's new-business waterfall journey.

One priority-routed cascade across the entire consumer-brand market, built on a single wedge: work that's impossible to ignore, without the holding-company tax.

Core insight: A brand needs an outside creative partner right now when there's an observable creative-demand trigger inside its marketing, brand, or retail org — a new brand leader, fresh funding, a hiring surge, a retail expansion. Rank those triggers by urgency, route the whole market on that one signal, and every message leans on Latitude being independent and free from holdco drag.

02

The waterfall approach

Build the strategy once, map the whole consumer-brand market to it, then keep adding leads for 11+ months without a rebuild.

first match wins 
1. IF not a proven-vertical consumer brand at mid-to-large scale -> OUT OF TAM (The qualifying gate: a consumer brand ($50M–$5B) in sportswear, outdoor, F&B, retail/CPG, consumer health, or consumer-facing industrial — Latitude's proven ICP.)
2. IF marketing / brand leader in role under 6 months -> TIER 1 — New Brand Leadership (A new leader re-evaluates agencies and wants a signature launch. The #1 agency-acquisition trigger and the hottest wedge for the anti-holdco pitch.)
3. IF ELSE recent funding (under 12 mo) OR hiring in-house creative / brand roles -> TIER 2 — Growth Surge (Demand is outrunning in-house capacity, and hiring is too slow to keep up.)
4. IF ELSE a retail / experiential expansion signal is present -> TIER 3 — Retail & Experiential (A physical brand experience must be designed at a level in-house rarely staffs for, against opening dates that won't move.)
5. IF ELSE any remaining proven-vertical consumer brand -> CATCH-ALL — Independence + Portfolio (Brand plateau and cost scrutiny; tired of the holdco tax. 100% market coverage, softest and broadest hook.)

Launch plan & thresholds

Phase-roll one tier at a time starting with new brand leaders; hold every tier at 1,500+ sends before judging.

launch first
01

Tier 1 first — New Brand Leadership. Highest intent, cleanest signal (Sales Nav tenure), fastest proof, and the anti-holdco pitch lands hardest with a leader still choosing their agencies.

02

Tier 3 second — Retail & Experiential. Latitude's most differentiated, least-commoditized capability, so reply quality is high even at lower volume.

03

Tier 2 third — Growth Surge. The largest signal-driven pool and strong compounding once the top of the funnel is tuned.

04

Catch-all last — the durable volume engine and 11-month base; turned on once the top tiers are sending clean.

05

Spin up the Force-for-Good / supplier-diversity side campaign in parallel once the core waterfall is stable.

06

Before send: collect real proof metrics and named case-study permissions from Latitude, and confirm who fulfills the teardown offers on a reply.

Scale / cut rules

1 positive reply / 150 contacts Perfect — scale that tier immediately.
1 / 300 Good — let it run.
1 / 450–500 Borderline — test a copy variant (vary the lead magnet or the notice-line angle).
Past 1 / 500, or under 1% reply after 1,500+ sends Cut the tier and rework. Never read a tier before 1,500 sends.
