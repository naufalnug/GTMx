Signal

The buying signal is how ready the plant is to invest

A company's vertical changes the noun in the email. The public readiness signal, a recall, an expansion, an open req, decides the campaign it lands in.

strongest signal
01

A recall or FDA 483 that cites a count, fill or labeling error is acute, public pain that traces straight to the packaging line, and to Aylward's single sharpest claim.

02

A new-facility or line-expansion announcement proves budget is allocated and a packaging line must be stood up soon, right the first time.

03

A live req for a packaging, automation or serialization role proves the plant is scaling a line by hand, exactly the labor Aylward's automation removes.

04

Packaging equipment leaves no web footprint, so the public record, recalls, expansions and job posts, is the only reliable at-scale intent signal. We invert on presence, never on absence.

05

Every solid-dose plant eventually replaces or upgrades a line, so no account is truly cold. The catch-all sweeps the rest.

Waterfall

One cascade routes the whole solid-dose market

Every solid-dose account flows top to bottom: quality-failure retrofit, capacity expansion, staffing the line, or catch-all. Most land in exactly one.

first match wins 
1. IF makes solid dose AND a recall / 483 / warning letter cites count, fill or labeling (last 18 mo) -> Tier 1 (Acute, FDA-visible pain the count-verified line removes)
2. IF makes solid dose AND announced a new facility, expansion or new solid-dose approval (last 12 mo) -> Tier 2 (Budget is live and a line must be stood up fast)
3. IF makes solid dose AND hiring a packaging, automation or serialization role -> Tier 3 (Scaling manual line labor by hand)
4. IF any remaining solid-dose manufacturer or contract packager -> Catch-all (An aging line is quietly costing giveaway and changeover)

Launch

launch first
01

Launch Tier 1 first from the openFDA recall and 483 pull of solid-dose sites citing count, fill or labeling. Highest intent, sharpest pain, fastest proof. Pool the US and Europe with an 18-month lookback to clear enough volume.

02

04

Open the catch-all last, once the higher tiers have proven copy and warmed the sending infrastructure, on the buyer's-guide CTA.

05

Do not light up a tier until its pull delivers 1,500+ contacts. Below that, results are not statistically readable. If Tier 1 dips below 1,500, run it as a rolling side-trigger rather than a standing tier.

Scale / cut rules

1 positive reply / 150 contacts Perfect. Scale the tier hard.
1 / 300 Good. Let it run untouched.
1 / 450-500 Borderline. Test one copy or lead-magnet variant.
Worse than 1 / 500 or <1% after 1,500 sends Cut the tier or rework it from the trigger up.
