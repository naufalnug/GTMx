The iVentures Wealth outbound journey.

One priority-routed cascade that turns India's public filing layer into a origination engine for a SEBI-registered adviser.

Core insight: You cannot buy a list of Indians holding ₹10Cr. Wealth is invisible — which is why every wealth manager in India competes on the same three capacity-bound channels: referrals, events, and poaching relationship managers. But the events that create wealth are filed and public. A DRHP names its selling shareholders. MCA21 publishes every private company's balance sheet and every director's date of birth. ESOP buybacks are announced as employer-brand news. So we never target net worth. We target proximity to a filed event that forces a wealth decision.

The signal

Wealth is invisible. The events that create it are filed, public and structured — so the event becomes the signal.

strongest signal

It is observable. SEBI filings, MCA21 accounts and buyback announcements are public record — no assumption is ever made about anyone's private finances.

It is defensible. Referencing a public filing is a professional courtesy; referencing someone's net worth is surveillance. For this audience, that distinction decides whether you get a reply.

02

The routing logic

Every contact is tested top-to-bottom and routes to the first tier it matches. A mandatory catch-all guarantees full coverage.

first match wins 
1. IF A liquidity event closed or was announced in the last 12 months -> Tier 1 — Post-Liquidity (An irreversible sum has just landed. Capital-gains exposure is live, there is no deployment plan, and every caller is paid by the product they are recommending.)
2. IF Not Tier 1, and the contact is NRI, OCI or recently returned to India -> Tier 2 — Cross-Border (Wealth is split across two tax regimes, Indian folios went dormant on departure, and no single adviser is licensed on both sides.)
3. IF Not Tier 1 or 2, and reserves ≥ ₹50Cr, or Series B+ within 18 months, or a new CFO within 6 months -> Tier 3 — Idle Treasury (A large surplus is sitting in current accounts and rolling FDs with no written policy, so the CFO is personally carrying an undocumented decision.)
4. IF Not Tier 1, 2 or 3, and two or more same-surname directors sit on one board with the eldest 55+, or three or more group entities share common directorship -> Tier 4 — Succession (Ownership grew around the business rather than being designed. The transfer happens whether or not it is planned.)
5. IF Everything remaining -> Tier 5 — The Unadvised Earner (High income, no strategy. Four to six relationship managers each see one slice, and nobody in the picture is paid by the client.)

Launch & measurement

Compliance clears first, then tiers roll in order of intent density — throttled to what the advisory bench can absorb.

launch first
01

Week 0 — Compliance first. Submit all 15 templates to BASL for prior approval under the SEBI advertisement code. Nothing sends until they clear, so this sits on the critical path. Lock the merge-field list to FirstName, Company, EventType, EventDate, Geography and RoleTitle — no freely generated sentence ever enters an approved template.

02

Week 0 — Restate the two blocking claims. The ₹2Cr inheritance-tax figure needs rewording (India abolished estate duty in 1985 and levies no inheritance tax), and the 8–12% rental yield and 15–20% appreciation figures must be removed from any outbound use.

03

Weeks 1–2 — Launch Tier 1, Post-Liquidity. Smallest list, sharpest trigger, fastest read on whether the fee-only positioning lands.

04

Weeks 3–4 — Launch Tier 2, Cross-Border. The commercial priority: largest high-intent tier and the one segment where a SEBI registration is hardest for an offshore competitor to match.

05

Weeks 5–6 — Launch Tier 3, Idle Treasury, once the MCA21 reserves scrape is delivering clean filings at volume.

06

Weeks 7–8 — Launch Tier 4, Succession, written with restraint and measured on meetings booked rather than reply rate.

07

Week 9+ — Open Tier 5, the catch-all, and scale to the volume the advisory bench can genuinely absorb.

08

Ongoing — Archive every sent variant for five years as the advertisement code requires, and layer the four side campaigns (post-Budget, new CXO, recognition, return-to-India) on top of the cascade as time-boxed sends.

Scale / cut rules

1 positive reply per 150 contacts Excellent. Scale this tier hard.
1 per 300 Good. Let it run untouched.
1 per 450–500 Borderline in most markets, acceptable here. Hold and test a single copy variant.
Worse than 1 per 800, or under 1% reply after 1,500 sends Underperforming. Rework the tier.
Why the cut line is looser than standard Our usual rule cuts past 1 per 500, calibrated to software where a deal is worth a few thousand dollars a year. A single ₹10Cr relationship at roughly 1% is about ₹10 lakh of recurring annual revenue, running a decade or more. At 15% conversion from positive reply to onboarded client, one client per 3,300 contacts still returns exceptionally. Judge these tiers on economics, not on reply-rate convention.
The real constraint Capacity, not volume. A 20-person advisory team cannot properly onboard 400 new families a year. Throttle sends to what the bench can absorb, and set that number before launch.
