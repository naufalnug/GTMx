01

The routing signal

Route on how the institution currently runs alumni engagement and online giving, read straight off its public web presence. No enrichment spend, no data vendor.

strongest signal
01

It is observable on 100% of the market. Alumni portals live on predictable subdomains, giving day microsites publish their totals, donation forms reveal the CRM, and advancement job reqs name it outright.

02

It maps directly to proven spend. A school on Hivebrite has budget and a renewal date. A school on a third-party giving day has proven it will pay for digital fundraising. A school hiring has an open budget line right now.

03

The pain is inferable from the trigger, never invented. A separate community platform implies engagement data that never reaches the record. A rented giving day implies a manual reconciliation window afterwards.

04

It survives the vertical split. Higher ed, K-12 independent schools and healthcare foundations all route through the same five tiers. Institution type is a vocabulary swap, not a second strategy.

02

The data matrix and routing spec

Eleven sources deliver the market and the signal in one pull. Thirteen fields per account. First match wins, top to bottom.

first match wins 
1. IF is_almabase_customer = TRUE -> Suppress (Existing customers and open opportunities never enter the send list. Detected via the Almabase platform footprint plus a CRM export.)
2. IF alumni_platform_detected is not none -> Tier 1 - Platform Switch (A competitor alumni platform is the strongest possible proof of budget, category belief and a renewal date. It outranks everything below it.)
3. IF giving_platform_detected is not in (none, blackbaud-native) -> Tier 2 - Giving Day Silo (A third-party giving day proves they will spend on digital fundraising, and the microsite publishes dollars and donor counts for free personalisation.)
4. IF open_advancement_role = TRUE and posted within 60 days -> Tier 3 - New Headcount (An open req is an open budget line right now. Job descriptions also name the CRM, which hands you the merge field.)
5. IF crm_detected is in (renxt, blackbaud-crm, salesforce, ellucian) -> Tier 4 - CRM-Rich, Engagement-Poor (They own the system of record but engagement is do-it-yourself. Perfect technical fit, latent pain, no competitor to displace.)
6. IF no condition above matched -> Tier 5 - Catch-All Peer Benchmark (Everything else with an alumni base and an advancement function. Broadest copy, softest CTA, 100% coverage.)

Launch plan and thresholds

Prove the thesis on the narrowest tier first. Five phases across twelve weeks, with reply-density rules that decide what scales and what gets cut.

launch first
01

Weeks 1-3, Tier 1 only, ~2,000 contacts. Fastest proof. The competitor takedown carries the strongest signal and validates the whole silo-and-sync thesis before spending on the long tail.

02

Weeks 3-6, add Tier 2, ~4,500 contacts. Giving day data is the richest personalisation in the strategy because the dollars and donor counts are public. Highest expected reply rate after Tier 1.

03

Weeks 6-9, add Tier 3, ~6,000 contacts. Turn on the rolling job board feed. It refreshes monthly, so it needs to start early enough to build a standing pipeline.

04

Weeks 9-12, add Tier 4, ~9,000 contacts. The largest qualified tier. Only open it once the copy is proven above, because volume at this scale punishes a weak thesis.

05

Week 12 onward, add Tier 5, full TAM. Catch-all at scale with the softest call to action.

06

Prerequisites before the first send: suppression list built from the Almabase footprint plus a CRM export; three to four secondary domains with three mailboxes each, fourteen-day warmup, 25-30 sends per mailbox per day; and the Tier 1 and Tier 2 lead magnets finished and fulfilment-ready.

07

Seasonality guard: do not judge a tier on data collected in the two weeks either side of a prospect's own giving day, mid-November to early January, or mid-June to mid-July. Advancement is unreachable in those windows and a good tier will read as a failing one.

Scale / cut rules

1 positive reply per 150 contacts Excellent. Scale the tier, add sending capacity, and pull more of this segment into the list.
1 per 300 Good. Let it run untouched. Do not optimise a working tier.
1 per 450 to 500 Borderline. Test one copy variant. Rotate the lead magnet first and hold everything else constant.
Worse than 1 per 500, or reply rate under 1% after 1,500 sends Cut the tier and rework the trigger. The problem is almost always the signal, not the sentence.
