Signal

The signal ladder is channel maturity

Every trade buyer routes by observable behavior: sells online plus a rival brand, sells online with a category gap, strong footfall but no e-shop, or simply relevant and unaware.

strongest signal
01

A detected rival compression or orthopedic brand proves the category already sells and the buyer already buys it.

02

An e-shop with a category gap proves the channel and audience exist, with margin leaking from a missing category.

03

High Google review counts with no e-shop prove real footfall where recommendations happen but resale margin is lost.

04

Every remaining relevant business can still be reached with the 100-year heritage story and a free sample.

Waterfall

One cascade routes every trade buyer

Each business is tested top-to-bottom on channel maturity and routes to exactly one wholesale campaign. The four verticals are the segmentation axis, not four campaigns.

first match wins 
1. IF has_eshop = true AND sells_compression_or_ortho = true AND competitor_brand_detected != none -> Tier 1 (Competitor Switch)
2. IF has_eshop = true AND competitor_brand_detected = none -> Tier 2 (Category Expansion)
3. IF has_eshop = false AND review_count >= 80 -> Tier 3 (In-Store Stocking)
4. IF every remaining relevant business in the TAM -> Catch-all (Heritage Trade Intro)

1 / 300 Good. Let it run and add leads steadily.
1 / 450-500 Borderline. Test a copy or lead-magnet variant.
