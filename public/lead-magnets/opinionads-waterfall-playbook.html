The routing

First match wins, and nothing falls through

Each account is tested top to bottom and lands in exactly one tier. The catch-all guarantees no lead is left out.

first match wins 
1. IF running_display_ads == FALSE -> Discard — not in market (The gate. If they aren't buying display, there is no impression to instrument and nothing to measure.)
2. IF legacy_measurement_evidence != none -> Tier 1 — Legacy Panel Displacement (They already bought the argument that brand lift matters. We aren't selling the category, we're selling a better vendor.)
3. IF NOT Tier 1 AND dsp_detected != none -> Tier 2 — Programmatic Scale, Zero Proof (Real budget committed to programmatic, graded on CTR and impressions. They can report delivery, not impact.)
4. IF NOT Tier 1/2 AND insights_function_present == TRUE -> Tier 3 — In-House Insights, Out-of-House Speed (A research function exists, which implies a panel dependency and a cycle too slow to steer live media.)
5. IF ELSE -> Tier 4 — Static Ads Don't Engage (catch-all) (All remaining verified display advertisers. Broadest signal, softest CTA, and the reason the cascade covers 100% of the market.)

The plan

Phase-rolled over 12 weeks

Tier 1 first because it produces the fastest honest read on message-market fit.

launch first
01

Weeks 1-3 — Tier 1 only, 1,500 to 2,000 contacts. Run the chaos test: methodology-led vs. cost-led, two genuinely different arguments against the same incumbent. Tier 1 goes first because it is the only tier where the prospect has already paid money to solve this exact problem — we are selling a better vendor, not the category.

02

Week 3 — First read. The chaos-test winner becomes the Tier 1 control. If a company writing six figures for panel studies won't take a free teardown of the methodology they're paying for, that is something worth knowing in week 3 rather than week 12.

03

Weeks 3-6 — Tier 2 opens at 3,000 contacts. Tier 1 keeps running untouched if it is holding 1-in-300 or better. Tests whether 'no incremental media cost' carries the reply on its own.

04

Week 6 — Cut any tier past 1-in-500. Rework it rather than tinkering with it. Two copy variants per tier, maximum.

05

Weeks 6-9 — Tier 3 opens at 2,500 contacts. Different buyer entirely — insights, not media. Watch for a different lead magnet winning here; it probably will.

06

Weeks 9-12 — Tier 4 catch-all opens at 5,000+. Volume tier, and it inherits whichever value prop travelled furthest in Tiers 1-3 rather than guessing.

07

Ongoing — the strategy does not get rebuilt. The lead agent keeps adding accounts to the same four tiers. Map the market once, build once, add leads forever.

Scale / cut rules

1 positive reply per 150 contacts Perfect. Scale the tier hard.
1 per 300 Good. Let it run untouched.
1 per 450-500 Borderline. Test one copy variant — vary the lead magnet first.
Worse than 1 per 500, or reply rate under 1% after 1,500+ sends Dead. Cut the tier and rework it.
