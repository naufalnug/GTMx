02

The waterfall approach

Build the strategy once, map the whole SMB market to it, then keep adding leads for 11+ months without rebuilding.

first match wins 
1. IF vertical not in target set OR headcount > 200 OR no live website -> OUT OF TAM (The qualifying gate: an SMB in a target vertical with a live site and a human doing sales — The Agency's ICP.)
2. IF hiring a revenue role, posted within 90 days -> TIER 1 — Hire-vs-System (Problem admitted, budget committed to the slowest, most expensive fix. Hottest wedge.)
3. IF ELSE running live Meta / IG ads -> TIER 2 — Lead-Leak (Paying for clicks and almost certainly leaking them with no instant follow-up.)
4. IF ELSE recently funded or expanding -> TIER 3 — 30-Day Buildout (Growth mandate and no GTM engine yet — about to duct-tape tools or over-spend on a custom build.)
5. IF ELSE any remaining qualifying SMB -> CATCH-ALL — Scorecard (Owner still running sales by hand. 100% TAM coverage; softest, broadest hook.)

Launch plan & thresholds

Phase-roll one tier at a time, founder-led on Tier 1; hold every tier at 1,500+ sends before judging.

launch first
01

Tier 1 first — highest intent, budget already committed to the slow fix, cleanest ROI story, and the founder-led send turns The Agency's core positioning into proof.

02

Tier 2 second — large, always-on, and the “same ad budget, more booked revenue” math is visceral for anyone already spending on Meta.

03

Tier 3 third — smaller but urgent and well-funded; the 30-day-vs-6-month story sells itself to a team with a growth mandate.

04

Catch-all last — the volume tier; needs the biggest list and the softest scorecard CTA, so it goes once the sending motion is proven.

05

Wire the reply-side automation so every positive reply is caught by The Agency's own AI agent and booked instantly — the demo inside the demo.

06

Start with fitness, med spas, and home services — high volume, fast sales cycles, clear follow-up pain.
