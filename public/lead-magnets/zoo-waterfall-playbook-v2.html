Two signals, one fit: hardware teams doing CAD who've already bet on AI.

A scrollable GTMx strategy artifact for routing the hardware market on the one intersection that predicts a fit — teams doing CAD design who are actively investing in AI to cut time and cost.

Core insight: A CAD tool named in a job post proves a company does hardware design — but on its own it doesn't predict a fit, because Zoo automates repetitive design work, it doesn't rip and replace the CAD that handles complex assemblies. The signal that predicts a fit is an intersection: a company doing CAD design AND actively investing in AI to cut time and cost in engineering. The CAD hire proves the workflow exists; the AI initiative proves they're ready to put an agent to work inside it. Fit lives where those two overlap.

Waterfall

One cascade routes on the overlap

Every hardware account flows top to bottom by how strongly it shows both signals: the full CAD-plus-AI intersection, a partial signal, CAD-only, or catch-all.

first match wins 
1. IF does CAD design (design hire or CAD tool named) AND an AI initiative aimed at engineering (an AI/ML or automation hire, a mandate to cut design time/cost, or an AI-tied raise) -> Tier 1 (The full intersection — already using AI to cut time/cost, with the design workflow to point it at)
2. IF does CAD design AND an emerging AI-in-engineering signal (AI or automation language in the JD or careers page, or a single recent AI/ML hire), no full initiative yet -> Tier 2 (Half the overlap — ready to be shown AI can take the repetitive load)
3. IF confirmed CAD / hardware design work, no AI signal surfaced -> Tier 3 (Could be a fit — needs educating that AI automates the repetitive part)
4. IF any remaining hardware company in TAM -> Catch-all (Self-serve; automate the grunt work when they're ready)
