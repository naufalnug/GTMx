Waterfall

How leads route

Each practice is tested top-to-bottom and lands in the first tier it matches, so most sit in exactly one campaign.

first match wins 
1. IF Running Meta or Google ads now -> Tier 1 — Active Spender (Proven willingness to spend on acquisition; sharpest, most urgent pain.)
2. IF Else: 100+ reviews, OR 4.7 stars+ with 60+ reviews, OR 2+ locations -> Tier 2 — Reputation-Led (Established and busy on word-of-mouth, but not proactively acquiring or reactivating.)
3. IF Else: has a website AND online booking software -> Tier 3 — Digitally Set-Up (Tools are in place but there is no system driving consistent new-patient flow.)
4. IF Else: everything remaining -> Tier 4 — Under-Marketed (catch-all) (Invisible online, relying on walk-ins — 100% TAM coverage, no practice excluded.)

Launch

Launch plan & thresholds

Phase-rolled starting with the sharpest pain, with pre-agreed rules to scale what works and cut what doesn't.

launch first
01

Phase 1 — Launch Tier 1 (Active Spender): proven spenders, sharpest pain, fastest read on signal in ~2 weeks across ~2,200 leads.

02

Phase 2 — Roll Tier 3 (Digitally Set-Up): the largest digitally-reachable pool; the 'add the system' pitch converts well.

03

Phase 3 — Roll Tier 2 (Reputation-Led): higher-value practices via the reactivation angle.

04

Phase 4 — Roll Tier 4 (Under-Marketed) once the lead magnets are validated on the sharper tiers.

05

Throughout: two copy variants per tier max; chaos-test a fresh tier, then narrow to scientific tests once a winner emerges.

Scale / cut rules

1 positive reply / 150 contacts Excellent — scale this tier hard.
1 / 300 Good — let it run.
1 / 450-500 Borderline — test a copy variant (lead magnet or first line).
Worse than 1 / 500, or <1% reply after 1,500 sends Underperforming — cut and rework the tier.
