Signal

The signal every site publishes on purpose

A construction site cannot keep its booking process secret. It has to tell forty trade partners how to get a truck through the gate, so it gets published. That published artifact is both the TAM and the routing signal, pulled from the same source.

strongest signal
01

Sites must publish delivery instructions to their trade partners and suppliers, so the booking method is observable from outside without any enrichment step.

02

The artifact they publish maps directly to maturity: a competitor portal, a spreadsheet, a job ad, a filed traffic plan, or nothing at all.

03

Pain is inferred straight from the trigger, never invented. An Excel booking sheet proves there is no live source of truth. It cannot mean anything else.

04

The signal refreshes itself. Projects break ground, job ads post and traffic plans get filed, so accounts move up the ladder on their own and each move is a genuinely new reason to make contact.

Waterfall

One cascade routes every congested gate

How they book deliveries today creates six exclusive tiers, from a competitor portal already in place, down through spreadsheets, job ads and filed traffic plans, to a catch-all nobody escapes.

first match wins 
1. IF competitor_vbs_detected != none -> Tier 1, Competitor Swap (A live booking portal proves budget, proves the problem is acknowledged, and proves a tool is already in place. The hottest signal available, so it is tested first.)
2. IF manual_booking_artifact != none -> Tier 2, The Spreadsheet Tier (A published PDF, Excel or Google Form is Veyor's tagline made literal. The problem is acknowledged and solved by hand, which means there is no live source of truth.)
3. IF logistics_role_hiring == true -> Tier 3, Headcount-as-Software (They are buying the solution as salary. The role is mostly radio and manual reconciliation, it does not scale past one site, and it leaves when the person does.)
4. IF ctmp_filed == true OR (project_value_usd > 20,000,000 AND project_urban_density == cbd) -> Tier 4, Mandated Constraint (The constraint is external and enforced. Council-mandated windows plus zero laydown space equals fines, stop-work risk, and no defensible audit trail.)
5. IF procore_or_autodesk_detected == true -> Tier 5, The Last Analog Workflow (Docs, RFIs and schedule are digitised, but Procore has no gate booking, so deliveries fall back to phone and paper. The weakest inference in the ladder, which is why it sits low.)
6. IF no condition above matched -> Tier 6, Catch-All (Congestion assumed but unproven. This tier is mandatory: it absorbs every detection failure, so an account we could not read still gets contacted rather than dropped.)

Launch

Launch the hottest signal first, freeze by week 12

Start with the competitor swap for fast proof, then the spreadsheet tier as the long-term workhorse. By week 12 the strategy freezes and the GTM engineer only adds leads.

launch first
01

Weeks 1-3. Launch Tier 1, Competitor Swap. Smallest list, highest intent, fastest proof, and competitor logo pages make it nearly free to build. If the positioning wins on the swap, every tier below inherits the confidence.

02

Weeks 3-6. Launch Tier 2, the Spreadsheet Callback. The hero tier: the tagline made literal, quoting the prospect's own published form back at them. Expect this to be the long-term volume workhorse.

03

Weeks 6-9. Launch Tiers 3 and 4 in parallel. Both are public-record driven and take longer to build, so start them once Tier 2 has proven the value prop.

04

Weeks 9-12. Launch Tier 5 and the Catch-All last. Highest volume, lowest intent, so the copy should arrive already sharpened by four tiers of real replies. The catch-all should be the most iterated tier, not the least.

05

Week 12 onward. Freeze the strategy. The GTM engineer only adds leads. Re-scrape the signal fields quarterly, because accounts migrate up the ladder on their own as projects break ground and job ads post, which means the waterfall re-tiers itself and the same account can be legitimately re-approached with a genuinely new reason.

Scale / cut rules

1 positive reply per 150 contacts Perfect. Scale the tier hard.
1 per 300 Good. Let it run untouched.
1 per 450 to 500 Borderline. Test one copy variant, varying the lead magnet or research angle only. Hold everything else constant.
Worse than 1 per 500, or reply rate under 1% after 1,500 sends Failing. Cut the tier and rework it.
Fewer than 1,500 sends Do not judge the tier at all. Below that number you are reading noise, not signal. Most programmes kill their best campaign in week two on a sample of 300.
