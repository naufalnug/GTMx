Signal

The buying signal is the developer's capital stage

Where a developer sits in the capital lifecycle, entitled, maturing, land-banked, or active, decides which Upside service opens the conversation. It is datable from public records, no cold enrichment.

strongest signal
01

A filed entitlement with no construction loan proves a committed project that needs capital now, and both facts are public.

02

A loan maturity date proves a refinance or recapitalization event is coming on a known clock, datable from CMBS and lender records.

03

A recent land purchase with nothing filed proves a pre-development project stalled before entitlement.

04

Every remaining active developer still has a next deal coming, so the catch-all captures the whole market.

Waterfall

One cascade routes the whole market

Every developer flows top to bottom: entitled but uncapitalized, facing a maturity wall, sitting on land, or the catch-all. Most land in exactly one campaign.

first match wins 
1. IF entitlement or major permit filed within 90 days AND no construction loan recorded -> Tier 1 (Entitled, not yet capitalized)
2. IF owns a CRE asset with a loan maturing within 18 months -> Tier 2 (Facing a maturity wall)
3. IF acquired a development parcel within 6-12 months AND no permit filed -> Tier 3 (Land banked, pre-entitlement)
4. IF any other active Northeast CRE developer or owner -> Catch-all (Next capital need is coming)
