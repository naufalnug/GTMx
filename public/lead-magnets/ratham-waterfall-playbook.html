The signal

One signal routes the entire market

How visible and how legally exposed a company's commute operation already is, judged entirely from public data.

strongest signal
01

It is published, not inferred. Indian JDs state shift timing and transport benefits verbatim, at enormous volume, for free.

02

It maps straight to liability. The OSH Code makes safe night transport for women (7PM-6AM) the employer's obligation, not the vendor's.

03

It is positive, never absent. We route on what a company says about itself, so a failed tier simply falls to the next condition.

04

It ladders cleanly, from "already paying a competitor and still escalating" down to "large enough to run transport, with nothing public to show for it."

The waterfall

Build it once, add leads forever

Every account is tested top-to-bottom and routes to the first tier it matches. The catch-all guarantees nothing is left behind.

first match wins 
1. IF headcount < 200 OR city not serviced -> OUT OF TAM (The coverage gate. An account has to be big enough to run transport and sit in one of Ratham's 15 cities to be serviceable at all.)
2. IF incumbent_etms != none OR active_transport_rfp == true -> TIER 1 — Displacement (They bought the category and still escalate. Budget is committed, the problem is admitted, and a renewal is a live wound.)
3. IF shift_type IN (night, rotational, us-shift, uk-shift) AND transport_benefit_stated == true -> TIER 2 — Night-shift liability (They published a commitment the OSH Code makes theirs to evidence, and almost none of them can evidence it.)
4. IF transport_role_signal == true -> TIER 3 — Headcount workaround (The open req is the confession. They're paying a salary to be the integration layer between four vendors and a spreadsheet.)
5. IF expansion_signal != none (last 6 months) -> TIER 4 — Scale / new-site (Commute always gets planned after the lease and the laptops. This is the window where the gap is real and known.)
6. IF esg_commitment IN (ev-fleet, scope3, net-zero) -> TIER 5 — EV / Scope 3 (They committed publicly. Commute is the Scope 3 line they're estimating because nobody measures it per trip.)
7. IF everything else -> CATCH-ALL (43% of the TAM. Multi-vendor chaos with no public signal, invisible until it breaks. Never skip the catch-all.)

The launch

Phase-rolled, read at 1,500 sends

Tier 2 goes first — the Naukri scrape is the backbone, so it's live on day one at full volume.

launch first
01

Weeks 1-2 — Tier 2 only. The biggest signal-carrying tier at 24,500 contacts, the sharpest inferred pain, and the cheapest to source. Normally the highest-intent tier launches first; here it doesn't, because the Naukri JD scrape is the backbone of the entire system and is live on day one at full volume.

02

Weeks 3-4 — add Tier 1. Smaller list, highest intent, but it depends on app-store review and procurement scraping that takes a sprint to build. We launch what's already standing, and Tier 1 follows two weeks later.

03

Weeks 5-6 — add Tiers 3 and 4, once the Naukri and LinkedIn pipeline is stable and proven.

04

Weeks 7-8 — add Tier 5 and the Catch-all. Broadest and softest, and they run forever underneath everything else.

05

In parallel from day one — warm 4-6 secondary domains at 20-25 sends per inbox per day. Warmup has a two-week lead time, so it starts alongside the list build, never after it.

06

Send Tuesday to Thursday, 10:00-11:30 or 15:00-16:30 IST. Avoid Mondays — that's escalation day for admin teams, the one day they're least able to read a cold email.

07

Layer LinkedIn connect-and-note on Tiers 1-3 only. Indian admin and facilities buyers are unusually active on LinkedIn, and a soft connect two days ahead of the email measurably lifts replies. WhatsApp stays strictly post-reply.

08

Two things needed from Ratham to go live: confirm the 15 operating cities and any industry exclusions, and confirm whether Highradius, Bayer and Rolls-Royce are cleared for cold copy or must stay anonymised.

Scale / cut rules

1 positive reply / 150 contacts Perfect. Scale that tier immediately.
1 / 300 Good. Let it run untouched.
1 / 450-500 Borderline. Test one copy variant, one variable at a time.
Past 1 / 500, or under 1% reply after 1,500+ sends Cut the tier and rework the trigger.
Under 1,500 sends Read nothing. Not enough signal to tell a working tier from an unlucky one.
