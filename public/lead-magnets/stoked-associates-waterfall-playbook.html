Signal

The buying signal is office space-pressure

A company's sector changes the noun in the email. How hard its space is being squeezed decides the campaign it lands in.

strongest signal
01

A signed lease or a relocation post proves a fit-out decision is live right now, against a hard move-in date.

02

A recent funding round proves the budget and the growth that force a bigger, better office within months.

03

A hiring surge proves the current floor is already under strain before anyone has said a word.

04

A public ESG or net-zero commitment proves intent the physical office does not yet reflect.

05

Every remaining office still faces a fit-out or renewal decision on its lease cycle, so no account is truly cold.

Waterfall

One cascade routes the whole market

Every MY/SG office flows top to bottom: moving, just funded, bursting, ESG-committed, or catch-all. Most land in exactly one.

first match wins 
1. IF move_signal != none -> Tier 1 (A fit-out is needed now)
2. IF funding_recency = <12mo -> Tier 2 (Space expansion imminent)
3. IF headcount +20% (6mo) OR 10+ open roles -> Tier 3 (The floor is already strained)
4. IF esg_page_detected OR sustainability lead present -> Tier 4 (Office doesn't match the ESG story)
5. IF any remaining MY/SG office -> Catch-all (Every lease expires eventually)

Launch

Launch on intent, then widen the market

Start with confirmed moves, add the ESG scrape, turn on funding and hiring triggers, then open the catch-all.

launch first
01

Launch Tier 1 first from the property-news and relocation-post pull. Highest intent, fastest proof, cleanest story. Run one variant until it clears enough volume.

02

Launch Tier 4 next once the ESG website scrape is ready. The most differentiated angle, easy to detect at scale, and tied to a specific Stoked service line.

03

Turn on Tier 2 from Crunchbase, e27 and Tech in Asia, sent within weeks of a raise going public.

04

Layer Tier 3 from Sales Navigator growth and careers-page signals once Tiers 1 and 4 reveal which angle lands.

05

Open the catch-all last, once higher tiers have proven copy and warmed the sending infrastructure.

06

Do not light up a tier until its pull delivers 1,500+ contacts. Below that, results are not statistically readable.

Scale / cut rules

1 positive reply / 150 contacts Perfect. Scale the tier hard.
1 / 300 Good. Let it run untouched.
1 / 450-500 Borderline. Test one copy or lead-magnet variant.
Worse than 1 / 500 or <1% after 1,500 sends Cut the tier or rework it from the trigger up.
